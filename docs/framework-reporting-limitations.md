# Framework reporting limitations

Framework reports describe endpoint evidence coverage and traceability. They are
not certifications and must not be interpreted as legal, regulatory, audit, or
organization-wide compliance determinations.

The report deliberately separates:

- mapping coverage: controls with at least one mapping;
- validated mapping coverage: controls with a human-reviewed mapping;
- traceability coverage: controls linked by either provisional or validated
  mappings;
- formal assessment coverage: evaluated controls divided by formally assessable
  controls with validated mappings;
- technical automation coverage: fully automated technical controls divided by
  all technical controls;
- assessment coverage: evaluated controls divided by controls that are
  assessable with validated mappings;
- satisfied assessable controls: satisfied controls divided by evaluated,
  assessable controls.

CSA does not publish a generic "overall compliance percentage" for packs that
include procedural or organizational scope. Manual and unmapped controls remain
visible. Provisional mappings are displayed but excluded from formal status.

Point-in-time collector evidence cannot prove continuous enforcement, governance,
documented exceptions, compensating controls, organization-wide deployment, or
legal applicability. A qualified reviewer must combine CSA output with policy,
process, interview, and other audit evidence.

Reports display pack status, validated and provisional counts, evaluation mode,
formal assessment availability, source release/reference/retrieval date, digest,
and review state. `REVIEW_REQUIRED` results carry bilingual warnings and are
excluded from formal assessment. Local source paths are redacted. The Microsoft
guidance and NIS2 traceability packs always display their pack-specific
disclaimers.
